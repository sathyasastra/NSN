About Me

I am an Assistant Professor (Grade III) in the School of Mechanical Engineering at SASTRA Deemed University, Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. I have been serving SASTRA since June 2016, and was promoted to my current grade in September 2021. Prior to this, I served as an Assistant Professor at Saranathan College of Engineering, Tiruchirappalli (2012–2016). I completed my Ph.D in Mechanical Engineering in September 2020 under the guidance of Dr. N. Baskar, and hold an M.Tech in Advanced Manufacturing from SASTRA University (CGPA 8.88, Distinction) and a B.E. in Mechanical Engineering from Saranathan College of Engineering (84%, Distinction).

My research spans two interconnected themes: (1) advanced composite materials — particularly Glass Fibre Reinforced Polymer (GFRP) composites, characterised through experimental tensile, hardness, and drop-weight impact testing, and (2) machining science — investigating surface-textured cutting tools, nanoparticle-filled lubricant pockets, and multi-objective optimisation of machining parameters using evolutionary algorithms and statistical methods (RSM, GRA, TOPSIS, Genetic Algorithm, PSO, Firefly).

My recent work combines these strengths with data-driven modelling: I am developing the GFRP Virtual Tensile Lab, a Streamlit web application that uses Gaussian Process Regression (GPR) and Support Vector Regression (SVR) — evaluated via Leave-One-Out Cross-Validation — to predict Young's Modulus, UTS, failure strain, and toughness from composite composition inputs.

I am a Life Member of the Indian Society of Systems for Science & Engineering (ISSE – LM04432) and the Indian Welding Society (L01806). I have received two Best Paper Awards at international conferences hosted by NIT Tiruchirappalli, and was honoured as Mentor of the Year 2025 by FoSSEE, IIT Bombay for contributions to open-source engineering education. I am currently guiding a Ph.D scholar (S. Balaganesh) and have successfully completed two SASTRA Prof. T.R.R. Research Seed Fund grants.

Sustainable Polymer Composites

Developing eco-conscious fibre-reinforced composites using natural and recycled fibres — including pineapple leaf, glass, and hybrid systems — with a focus on reducing environmental impact while meeting structural performance requirements.
